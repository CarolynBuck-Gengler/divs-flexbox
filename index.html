During the pandemic, one thing our family has really gotten into is bicycling. It started in March when we just tried to go for a walk everyday, for exercise and to take a break. We started with short walks but soon built it up to an hour. When the weather got warm enough, we swapped our walking shoes for our bicycles, because we could go so much farther! Now we get in 4-5 hour-long rides each week, trying to get to different parts of our city; on Sundays we go nearly 2 hours. We always go early in the morning to avoid the heat later in the day.

Another thing I've taken up during this time is baking, especially bread. I was never a bread baker before, but I found recipes that didn't require kneading, and bake the bread in my Dutch oven. I've been working with one main recipe, trying to perfect my execution of it, and then branching out with greater proportions of whole wheat flour, and even adding herbs and seeds. I'm also trying other recipes such as a dill-cottage cheese bread, and a honey whole wheat bread. I even got myself a new smaller Dutch oven. It seems like a lot of people are doing this, because some ingredients (e.g. flour and yeast) have been hard to find; I still haven't been able to get vital wheat gluten.

Lastly, in the evenings we've been binging a lot of movies and TV shows we missed in the past. We got Disney+ and CBS All Access to see The Mandelorian, Clone Wars, Picard, Star Trek: Discovery, and Star Trek: Enterprise; and a couple weeks ago were thrilled to be able to watch Hamilton. We also watched several of the Andrew Lloyd Weber musicals when they were aired, and even some ballet versions of Romeo and Juliet.
